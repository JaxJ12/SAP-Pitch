Partnership Proposal 2027

SAP has a gap.
Baylor fills it.

There is no major SAP presence in college athletics anywhere in the American South. Baylor sits at the geographic and commercial heart of the fastest-growing business market in the US — and the timing has never been better.

Why now

Four reasons this
deal makes sense.

Every one of these angles has been validated by market data, SAP's own stated priorities, and Dan Fleetwood's fact-find conversation.

01
Southern market gap
SAP's sponsorship portfolio is concentrated in the Northeast US, Europe, and globally — Bayern Munich, Mercedes F1, NHL, Ryder Cup, New Zealand All Blacks. There is zero college athletics presence in the American South. Texas is one of the fastest-growing business markets in the US, and SAP has a Dallas office and significant Southwest customer base with no local sports brand presence to show for it.
200+ companies moved HQ to Texas in 5 years
02
I-35 corridor advantage
Waco sits halfway between Dallas (90 miles north) and Austin (100 miles south) — two of the top five fastest-growing metro economies in the country. The I-35 corridor is home to hundreds of mid-market companies in the $500M to $2B range — the exact segment Dan Fleetwood identified as SAP's primary growth target. A Baylor sponsorship puts SAP's brand in front of these decision-makers year-round.
Dallas + Austin = top 5 US growth metros
03
Jersey patch first-mover
The NCAA approved jersey patch sponsorships starting August 1, 2026 — perfectly aligned with SAP's 2027 planning horizon. SAP would be the first major enterprise technology company to put its logo on a Power 4 college jersey. UNLV signed for $11M over 5 years. LSU and Arkansas have already set new benchmarks. Baylor as a Big 12 program represents a top-tier first-mover opportunity in the most valuable college sports market in the country.
Big 12 patches valued $3.5M to $6M per year
04
First real US collegiate partnership
SAP's past US college work has been limited to brand-only plays near their Pennsylvania HQ (Villanova) and an academic institute (Stanford's Hasso Plattner Institute). Neither represents the kind of deep, collaborative athletics partnership Dan Fleetwood described as the gold standard. Baylor offers the first opportunity to build a reference story, showcase technology in an athletic operations context, and speak to SAP's values in a new market.
SAP's first real US collegiate athletics story

Client Needs Analysis

What we heard from SAP.
How Baylor delivers.

Based on our fact-find conversation with Dan Fleetwood, SAP Head of Global Sponsorships.

What SAP told us
How Baylor delivers
Pillar 1 — Brand and purpose
Authentic storytelling over logo slaps
SAP wants a partner they are proud to stand beside with shared values and genuine integration — not just logo visibility.
"It's not just a logo slap... how are we walking hand in hand in their success?"
Our answer
Values-aligned, first-of-its-kind partner
Baylor's faith-based, community-first values align naturally with SAP's purpose-driven brand. This would be SAP's first real US collegiate athletics story to tell — not a repeat of existing partners.
Pillar 2 — Most critical
Technology showcase is everything
Partners must USE SAP software. Dan cited the NHL iPad coaching app and 49ers real-time war room as the model for what a great partnership looks like.
"What business problems are you trying to solve? How can we help you run better?"
Our answer
Baylor as SAP's athletic tech showcase
Baylor can integrate SAP software into athletic operations — analytics platforms, ticketing systems, facilities management, and athlete performance data — creating a reference story SAP can use at Sapphire and TechEd.
Pillar 3 — Demand and sales
B2B decision-maker access is the goal
SAP uses hospitality to bring prospects in, show SAP technology in action, and convert that 10-15 minute experience into pipeline and follow-up meetings.
"Who are the business decision-makers in your fan base and how do you engage them?"
Our answer
37% of fans are biz decision-makers
Baylor fans are 3x more likely to be senior executives and 22% more likely to be business decision-makers than the general population. Corporate suites at McLane Stadium give SAP the B2B touchpoints they need on the I-35 corridor.
Growth target
Mid-market is SAP's priority
SAP is moving beyond Fortune 1000 into the $500M to $2B corporate segment and needs to shed the stigma of being "only for big business."
"There's a preconceived notion that we're only for big business... we're trying to shed that."
Our answer
Gateway to the Texas mid-market
The Dallas-Waco-Austin corridor is packed with exactly the $500M to $2B companies SAP is targeting. Over 200 brands relocated their HQ to Texas in the last five years — Oracle, Tesla, SpaceX, Chevron, Charles Schwab.
Pillar 4 — Employee engagement
Talent recruitment and employee pride
SAP uses partnerships for young talent recruitment programs, team rewards, and giving employees stories to tell and reasons to feel proud of their company.
"We use it for rewarding folks, team outings, and young talent recruitment programs."
Our answer
20,800+ students — SAP's talent pipeline
Baylor's student base and NIL programs give SAP a direct pipeline for young talent recruitment. SAP can engage students through sponsorship events, panels, and campus activations tied to the partnership.
