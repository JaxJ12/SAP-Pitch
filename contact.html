SAP Strategic Pillars — Why This Partnership Works

Pillar 01

Brand Awareness & VIK Integration

  • Value-in-Kind (VIK): Baylor Athletics formally adopts SAP Analytics Cloud, replacing legacy backend systems. SAP earns exclusive category ownership and a live Power 4 reference story.
  • Sapphire & TechEd: Baylor becomes a featured case study at SAP's global conferences — extending the brand far beyond Central Texas to a worldwide B2B audience.
  • No Competitor Conflict: Microsoft's Big 12 Surface tablet deal is a conference-level hardware agreement. SAP enters as Baylor's exclusive school-level enterprise software partner — a completely separate category.
  • Reference Story Engine: Hankamer School of Business + S3E co-author ongoing Sapphire/TechEd presentations, giving SAP a credible academic proof point for enterprise sales.

Pillar 02

Mid-Market Demand Generation

  • I-35 Corridor Lock: Waco–Dallas–Austin is the highest concentration of SAP's $500M–$2B target companies in Texas. Baylor owns this corridor at the executive level — no other property does.
  • Executive Huddle: 12 Baylor Club B2B events per year put SAP in a room with CFOs, CTOs, and COOs in a captive, premium setting. Modeled on the 49ers' proven executive engagement playbook.
  • In-App B2B Funnel: "Learn How SAP Powers This" in-app module routes educated fans directly to SAP's mid-market sales team. Every attendee becomes a potential prospect.
  • Market Comp: UNLV (Group of 5) secured $2.2M/yr for a jersey patch. Baylor as a Power 4 school with a premier business program commands a measurable premium above that floor.

Pillar 03

Sustainable & Always-On Partnership

  • Multi-Year Compounding Value: Year 1 proves the model. Year 2 scales the jersey patch. Year 3 produces the complete Sapphire/TechEd case study. Each season stacks new executives, new data, and deeper integration.
  • Always-On Content Engine: SAP is named in every Baylor Athletics press release, radio broadcast, TV coverage, and digital story — 365 days a year. This is not a game-day logo. It is a year-round media platform.
  • Co-Branded Social Media: Bi-weekly "How SAP Powers Baylor Game Day" content across X, Instagram, and LinkedIn reaches Baylor's 500K+ combined following with B2B-relevant storytelling.
  • ESG & Sustainability: SAP's environmental initiatives co-branded through Baylor's green stadium operations and community programs — global brand purpose with local, measurable proof.

Pillar 04

Employee Engagement & Talent Pipeline

  • S3E & University Alliance: Baylor's Center for Sports Strategy and Sales becomes an official SAP University Alliance partner, connecting top Hankamer business students directly to SAP's talent acquisition pipeline.
  • Employee Hospitality: Dedicated SAP employee perks packages — premium suite access at McLane Stadium and Foster Pavilion — drive internal engagement and culture well beyond the office.
  • Hankamer Joint Research: Co-produced faculty case studies amplify SAP's thought leadership in the higher-education and live-event verticals for enterprise sales and recruiting purposes.
  • Next-Gen Advocates: Baylor students actively co-author SAP's Sapphire and TechEd conference presentations, building a sustained pipeline of SAP-certified graduates entering the workforce every year.

Executive recommendation

  • Recommend a year-one enterprise integration package across suites, keynotes, and SAP App intelligence as the primary approval decision.
  • Use the jersey patch as a separate premium rights conversation once SAP confirms interest in a flagship visibility asset.
  • This sequencing lowers risk, protects Baylor's most valuable inventory, and still gives SAP a credible first-year business case.

Why Baylor is the right property

  • Baylor combines Power 4 visibility, premium hospitality access, and a credible business environment through the Baylor Club.
  • The property supports both football visibility and Baylor basketball relevance, giving SAP a broader premium brand story.
  • The regional footprint aligns with SAP's need for executive access in Texas rather than generic national exposure.

Commercial structure and asset discipline

  • Enterprise integration platform: $1.5M–$2.0M annually — covers suites and executive hospitality, keynote event series, SAP App intelligence, LED signage, and co-branded digital content. This is the primary Year 1 ask.
  • Separate flagship valuation: jersey patch negotiated independently because first-mover scarcity and cross-sport visibility justify a premium track.
  • Minimum asset discipline: each activation should include at least five communication assets so the proposal demonstrates real execution planning, not just broad ideas.

What should be measured and reported

  • Use one reporting structure across the whole deal: audience identification, engagement behavior, survey feedback, and business follow-up.
  • Track hosted guest mix, executive meetings created, keynote attendance quality, app engagement, and premium brand recall.
  • Run baseline, mid-season, and post-season surveys to measure how fan and guest perception of SAP changes over time as the activations make the brand more useful and easier to understand.
  • Separate activity metrics from outcome metrics so SAP leadership can see both what happened and what commercial value it created.

Measurement ownership and operating model

  • Baylor should own the strategy, first-party data capture, and activation design rather than outsourcing the thinking.
  • Playfly should support sponsor reporting, survey execution, recap packaging, and quarterly dashboard delivery because they are the official commercial partner in the process.
  • SAP should receive a recurring executive dashboard with audience IDs, engagement trends, survey lift, and commercial follow-up summaries by activation.
  • The dashboard should be reviewed quarterly so leadership can decide whether to scale, refine, or hold the patch conversation based on real year-one proof.
