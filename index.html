The Case for Baylor

SAP has a gap.
Baylor fills it.

SAP's sponsorship portfolio spans the globe — but there's one region missing entirely. Texas is the fastest-growing business market in the US, and Baylor sits right in the middle of it.

01
Southern market gap
SAP's portfolio is concentrated in the Northeast US, Europe, and globally. There is zero college athletics presence in the American South — Baylor is the first-mover opportunity.
02
I-35 corridor advantage
Waco sits halfway between Dallas (90 miles north) and Austin (100 miles south) — two of the top five fastest-growing business metros in the country. Over 200 companies have moved HQ to Texas in the last five years.
03
Jersey patch — first mover
The NCAA approved jersey patch sponsorships starting August 2026. SAP can be the first major enterprise tech company on a Power 4 college jersey — a genuine PR story with national reach.
04
First US collegiate partnership
SAP's past college work has been brand-only or academic. Baylor offers the first real collaborative athletics partnership in the US — the kind Dan Fleetwood described as the gold standard.
